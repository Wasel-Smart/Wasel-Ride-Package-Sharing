# Wasel Financial Projections - Jordan Intercity Transport

## Market Context (Jordan 2024)
- Population: 11.3M across 12 governorates
- Major intercity routes: Amman-Irbid, Amman-Zarqa, Amman-Aqaba, Amman-Karak
- Current transport: JETT buses, service taxis, private cars
- GDP per capita: $4,405 USD
- Average monthly income: $367 USD

## Revenue Model Analysis - Intercity Focus

### 1. Intercity Ride Sharing Revenue
**Commission Structure**: 10-15% (lower than urban due to higher fares)
**Average intercity fare**: 8-25 JOD ($11-$35 USD) depending on distance
**Platform commission per ride**: 1.2-3.8 JOD ($1.70-$5.35)

**Key Routes & Pricing**:
- Amman-Irbid (85km): 8-12 JOD → Commission: $1.70-$2.55
- Amman-Zarqa (30km): 5-8 JOD → Commission: $1.00-$1.70
- Amman-Aqaba (330km): 20-25 JOD → Commission: $4.25-$5.35
- Amman-Karak (120km): 10-15 JOD → Commission: $2.10-$3.20

**Conservative Projections**:
- Month 1-6: 150 rides/day → 4,500 rides/month
- Month 7-12: 400 rides/day → 12,000 rides/month  
- Year 2: 800 rides/day → 24,000 rides/month

**Monthly Revenue (Intercity Rides)**:
- Months 1-6: 4,500 × $2.50 = $11,250/month
- Months 7-12: 12,000 × $2.50 = $30,000/month
- Year 2: 24,000 × $2.50 = $60,000/month

## Market Penetration Assumptions - Intercity Focus

### Realistic Market Share
- **Total intercity travelers**: ~50,000 daily trips in Jordan
- **Addressable market**: 20,000 trips (willing to use app-based service)
- **Year 1 target**: 2% market penetration (400 daily trips)
- **Year 3 target**: 4% market penetration (800 daily trips)

### Competition Reality - Different Landscape
- **JETT buses**: Government buses, fixed schedules
- **Service taxis**: Unorganized, cash-only, no booking
- **Private cars**: Expensive, limited availability
- **Wasel opportunity**: First organized intercity ride-sharing platform

## Intercity Advantages

### Market Gaps Wasel Fills
- **Scheduled departures**: Unlike random service taxis
- **Digital booking**: No waiting at stations
- **Price transparency**: Fixed, known fares
- **Safety**: Verified drivers, GPS tracking
- **Comfort**: Better vehicles than service taxis

### Lower Competition Risk
- **Uber/Careem**: Focus on urban markets only
- **Local competitors**: None in intercity space
- **Barriers to entry**: Requires different operational model

## Honest Assessment - Intercity Model

**Strengths**:
- **No direct competition**: First mover in organized intercity ride-sharing
- **Higher margins**: Longer trips, higher fares
- **Underserved market**: Current options are poor (service taxis, buses)
- **Package opportunity**: Businesses need intercity delivery
- **Scalable**: Can expand to neighboring countries (Syria, Iraq when stable)

**Challenges**:
- **Lower frequency**: People travel intercity less often
- **Seasonal demand**: Tourism, university schedules affect volume
- **Driver logistics**: Drivers need to return or find return passengers
- **Fuel costs**: Higher impact on longer routes
- **Weather dependency**: Desert routes affected by sandstorms

**Reality Check**: Intercity has lower volume but higher margins. Success depends on operational efficiency and solving the "return trip" problem for drivers.
