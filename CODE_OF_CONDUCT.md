# Code of Conduct

## Our standard

This repository should be a professional and respectful place to collaborate. Contributors, reviewers, and maintainers are expected to communicate clearly, disagree constructively, and keep the focus on improving the product and the code.

## Expected behavior

- Be respectful in code review, issue discussion, and direct feedback.
- Assume good intent, but challenge ideas and implementations when the evidence requires it.
- Keep technical discussions grounded in facts, tradeoffs, and reproducible behavior.
- Help new contributors understand the repository standards and verification workflow.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, insults, or discriminatory language
- Personal attacks or hostile review behavior
- Deliberate misrepresentation of results, risks, or authorship
- Sharing private credentials, personal data, or sensitive operational details in public threads

## Maintainer responsibilities

Maintainers may edit, remove, or reject comments, issues, pull requests, or other contributions that do not meet this standard. Repeated or severe violations may result in restricted participation.

## Reporting

If conduct in this repository becomes a problem, contact the maintainers through a private GitHub channel rather than a public issue. If the report also involves a security concern, use the process documented in [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md).
